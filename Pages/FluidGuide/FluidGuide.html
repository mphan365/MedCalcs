Auto-Pilot Maintenance Fluids

The following regimens are based on the assumption that the patient meets the following criteria:
  1. They are relatively normal medically and weigh ~70kg
  2. They are NBM
  3. They are euvolemic

Regimen 1
Bag 1: 0.9% NaCl + 30 mmol/L KCl (premixed)
Bag 2: 5% dextrose + 30 mmol KCl
Bag 3: 5% dextrose

Regimen 2
Bag 1: 0.18% NaCl + 4% dextrose + 30 mmol/L KCl (premixed)
Bag 2: 0.18% NaCl + 4% dextrose + 30 mmol/L KCl
Bag 3: 0.18% NaCl + 4% dextrose

Infusion Rates
Normal Person: 125 mL/hour (q8h - every 8 hours)
Smaller/Older person: 100 mL/hour (q10h)

Fluid Resus

Step 1: Fluid Bolus
  Initial 500mL bolus of Crystalloid Solution over less than 15 minutes

Step 2: Reassessment
  After fluid bolus, reassess for evidence of ongoing hypovolemia
  If patient still has signs of hypovolemia, give further 250-500mL bolus of Crystalloid Solution

Repeat this process until either the patient is no longer hypovolemic OR you have given 2000mL fluid. If after 2000mL the patient is still hypovolemic, call for senior help!
